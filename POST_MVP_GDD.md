## Jonathan's Followers (Ally)

Full playable ally faction. Noble, loyal, elite Benjamite warriors. Represents the friendship between David and Jonathan.

| Unit | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Jonathan | Commander | Saul's son, David's closest friend, brave archer |
| Loyal Guards | Infantry | Devoted to Jonathan |
| Elite Archers | Ranged | Crack shots, scattered Philistines at Gibeah |
| Scouts | Light | Fast, intel |
| Armor-Bearer | Infantry | Fought beside Jonathan at Geba, "the Lord is with us" |
| Shield-Bearer | Support | Carries large communal shield, protects commander |
| Benjamite Spearman | Elite Infantry | Fierce tribe, deadly with both bow and sling |

**Commander ability:** Jonathan provides adjacent allies precision bonus (higher hit chance on ranged attacks). Loyal Guards gain +1 defense when adjacent to Jonathan.

## Philistines (Enemy/Neutral)

Major enemy faction. David lives among them as a mercenary, then becomes their enemy. Their iron weapons and chariots give them military superiority.

| Unit | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Achish | Commander | Lord of Gath, complex relationship with David |
| Lord of the Philistines | Commander variant | One of five city-state rulers |
| Philistine Spearman | Infantry | Standard core battle line |
| Heavy Infantry | Heavy | Better armor, larger shields, slow but devastating |
| Archer | Ranged | Standard ranged support |
| Charioteer | Unique | Ultimate Philistine weapon, iron chariots |
| Champion | Elite | Standard-bearers, duelists, tradition of Goliath |

**Commander ability:** Achish provides adjacent allies +1 armor (reduces incoming damage by 1). Charioteers ignore difficult terrain when moving in a straight line.

## Post-MVP Commanders

These appear in later campaigns post-Saul's death. Each brings a distinct tactical identity.

### Joab

- Biblical notes: David's nephew (son of Zeruiah), commander of David's army. Ruthless but brilliant. Killed Abner and Amasa. Led army against Absalom's rebellion.
- Commander style: Aggressive, commander-focused. Units within 2 tiles of Joab gain +1 attack but -1 defense (reckless offense).
- Visual: Tall, gray-streaked beard, battle-scarred, bronze scale armor.

### Amasa

- Biblical notes: David's nephew (son of Jether/Abigail). Appointed by David as commander over Judah in Joab's place. Killed by Joab during Absalom rebellion aftermath.
- Commander style: Diplomatic, balanced. Provides +1 command card draw when units take damage (cautious commander).
- Visual: Honest earnest expression, bronze spear, appointed captain bearing.

### Asahel

- Biblical notes: Joab's younger brother. Renowned for extraordinary speed — "as one of the gazelles." Killed by Abner at Gibeon with the butt of a spear.
- Commander style: Speed-focused. Single units can move twice per turn, but cannot be the same unit as last turn (flanking specialist).
- Visual: Lean, light armor, wrapped cloak for swift movement, running stance.

Elite named warriors from David's story. Each performs a single legendary exploit. These are late-campaign hero units.

| Unit | Biblical Source | Notable Exploit |
|---|---|---|
| Josheb-basshebeth (Adino) | 2 Sam 23:8 | Killed 800 men at one time with his spear |
| Eleazar son of Dodai | 2 Sam 23:9-10 | Stood alone at Pas Dammim, hand clave to sword |
| Shammah son of Agee | 2 Sam 23:11-12 | Defended the field of lentils alone against Philistines |
| Abishai son of Zeruiah | 2 Sam 23:18 | Killed 300 with his spear, honored above the Thirty |
| Benaiah son of Jehoiada | 2 Sam 23:20-23 | Killed two Moabites, a lion in the snow, an Egyptian with his own spear |

**Hero unit rules (post-MVP):**
- Each Mighty Man is a single powerful unit with unique stats.
- Only one Mighty Man per army.
- Dies permanently if eliminated (no revival).
- Provides a unique commander-level aura to adjacent units.

## Co-Op / Multiplayer (Sunderfolk-Style)

### Architecture

- **Host build (PC/Steam):** Authoritative hex grid, full board view, runs the game engine.
- **Thin clients (mobile web):** Show only the connected player's hand and unit selection. No board state.
- **Transport:** Unity Game Server Hosting or equivalent relay service.
- **State sync:** Turn-based discrete events, not realtime. Only card plays and unit selections are transmitted.

### Why Sunderfolk-style works for this game

- Turn-based card structure maps cleanly to thin-client hand view.
- Each phone only needs: commander portrait, hand of cards (4 max), unit selection for card targets.
- TV/PC renders: hex grid, unit tokens, opponent hands as face-down cards, attack modifier reveal.

### Challenges

- `CardAbilityResolver` and `PlayerInputHandler` must be refactored from local-only to network authority.
- Unit state must be authoritative on host only.
- AI director must run on host and broadcast intent, not on client.

### Recommendation

Build **single-player MVP first** (Sprints 3–7). Once the core game is fun and balanced, add co-op as a network layer around the same state machine. Do not build networking while the mechanics are still unproven.

- **Hebron** — David anointed king of Judah.
- **Jerusalem** — Capture the Jebusite fortress.
