# Command Identity & Activation Framework
## Draft v0.2

---

# Core Command Philosophy

Command cards represent **relationships and battlefield communication**.

A commander does not simply make soldiers stronger.

A commander determines:

- Who responds?
- How quickly do they respond?
- How far does their influence reach?
- How coordinated is the action?

Different factions should feel different because they have different command structures.

---

# Three Command Systems

## 1. Formal Command

### Saul's Kingdom

Power comes from:

- rank
- officers
- formations
- drilled responses

Strength:
- reliable
- scalable
- predictable

Weakness:
- rigid

Example:

> Officer's Order  
> Activate up to 3 units within formation.

---

## 2. Personal Command

### David's Company

Power comes from:

- loyalty
- trust
- shared hardship
- personal relationships

Strength:
- flexible
- adaptive
- surprising

Weakness:
- less efficient at commanding large armies

Example:

> Follow Me  
> Activate David and one allied squad.

---

## 3. Independent Command

### Mighty Men / Heroes

Power comes from:

- reputation
- personal initiative
- exceptional skill

Strength:
- breaks normal rules

Weakness:
- limited scale

Example:

> With Me!  
> Activate this hero and any nearby allied squad.

---

# Wild Activation (Refined)

## Definition

Wild Activation allows a commander or elite unit to activate outside their normal command category.

It represents:

- battlefield instinct
- personal loyalty
- trusted relationships
- exceptional leadership

However, Wild Activation should have limits.

---

# Wild Activation Levels

## Level 1: Companion Activation

Activate:

- This unit
- One other friendly unit

Examples:

David:
- David + any allied squad

Mighty Man:
- Hero + nearby ally

---

## Level 2: Circle Activation

Activate:

- This unit
- Any allied squad within X spaces

Examples:

David:

> Activate David and any allied squad within 3 spaces of David's starting space.

This represents David's trusted followers.

---

## Level 3: Rally Activation

Rare.

Activate:

- Multiple different unit types

Examples:

> Activate David and up to导向 3 allied squads within command range.

This should be:

- rare
- high initiative cost
- high card value

---

# Why Starting Space Matters

The "starting space" idea creates interesting tactical decisions.

David chooses:

- stay near the army → stronger command influence
- move aggressively → risk losing influence

This creates a natural "command footprint."

David's location matters beyond combat.

David should have:

## High Flexibility
+
## Moderate Scale
+
## High Adaptability

Not:

"David gives everyone bonuses."

Instead:

"David always has someone who can answer the call."

# Important Balance Rule

A Wild Activation card should usually sacrifice one of:

## Scale

"Only one extra unit."

## Range

"Only nearby units."

## Frequency

"Rare card."

## Strength

"Activation without bonuses."

# Final Design Principle

A good command card should make the player say:

"Of course this person would do that."

Not:

"This is the strongest numerical option."

David should win because his people trust him.

Saul should win because his army obeys him.

The Philistines should win because they are trained.

The Amalekites should win because they are unpredictable.

The cards should tell that story.

---

# Design Separation

This document defines:

**WHY a command card exists.**

The Command Potential Formula defines:

**HOW MUCH that command card is worth.**

Both should be used together when creating unit decks.
