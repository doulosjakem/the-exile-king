# IDEAS — Future Concepts & Out-of-Scope Brainstorming

> This file captures ideas that are **out of scope for the MVP** but worth remembering for later. Everything here is optional and subject to the core question:
> *"Does this make the tactical decisions more interesting?"*

---

## Gameplay Features

### Counter-Attacks
- Specific units or equipment grant counter-attack on melee
- Only triggers if the unit survives the incoming attack
- Could be a perk on Iron shields or certain elite units

### Terrain & Environment
- **High ground** — ranged attacks from elevated tiles get +1 range or +1 damage
- **Rough terrain** — costs 2 movement to enter (instead of 1)
- **Cover** — units behind obstacles get defense bonus against ranged
- **Hazards** — fire, broken ground, etc.
- **Dynamic terrain** — slingers could create smoke screens, Camel Rider trample could leave rough terrain

### Morale System
- Units break and flee if allies die nearby
- Commander presence bolsters morale
- Could replace simple HP with a morale bar for some units
- Risk: adds complexity, might slow combat

### Fog of War
- Only visible tiles are those within sight range of your units
- Scouts could have extended sight
- Ambush mechanics become possible
- High dev cost for MVP

### Campaign Map
- Overworld map instead of linear node selection
- Resources to manage (food, supplies)
- Random events between battles
- Branching paths with risk/reward

### Equipment Crafting
- Combine materials from defeated enemies
- Upgrade weapons with special properties (e.g., flaming arrows, serrated blades)
- Risk: can become grindy

### Weather / Time of Day
- Night battles — reduced sight range
- Rain — ranged accuracy penalty
- Dust storms — movement penalties
- Could be scenario-specific rather than dynamic

## Factions & Campaigns

### The Mighty Men
- David's elite warriors from Scripture
- Each could be a unique hero unit with special abilities
- Recruitment could be tied to story events
- Candidates: Josheb-Basshebeth, Eleazar, Shammah, Abishai, Benaiah

### David vs Saul Campaign
- David as the hunted, not the hunter
- Stealth / evasion mechanics
- Saul's soldiers have better equipment but lower morale
- Could use a different victory condition (escape, not eliminate)

### Philistine Campaign
- Heavy infantry (Goliath's people)
- Chariots (new unit type)
- Different tactical feel — enemy is slower but hits harder
- Goliath as a boss unit (massive HP, special abilities)

### Amalekite Campaign Expansion
- More Amalekite unit types (camel archers, war dogs?)
- Deeper into nomadic territory — supply lines matter
- Ambush scenarios where Amalekites start hidden

## Unit & Combat Ideas

### Hero Units (beyond David)
- Each hero has a unique ability
- Heroes can't be recruited — earned through story or challenges
- Examples: Abishai (bodyguard — can take a hit meant for David), Benaiah (beast slayer — bonus vs elite units)

### Mounted Units
- Horses (future, for Philistines primarily)
- Camels (Amalekites)
- Chariots (Philistines — 2-unit carrier?)
- Mounted archers (Parthian shot mechanic)

### Siege Equipment
- Battering ram (destroys obstacles)
- Siege tower (slow, carries units)
- Catapult (area damage, inaccurate)
- Probably not fitting for David-era Israel, but possible for Philistine scenarios

### Unit Synergies
- "Shield Wall" — adjacent swordsmen get defense bonus
- "Volley" — multiple slingers targeting the same tile get +1 damage each
- "Spear Wall" — spearmen in formation get bonus against charges

## Progression Ideas

### Skill Trees
- David gains experience and can spend points on abilities
- Branching choices (e.g., "Inspire" vs "Rally" vs "War Cry")
- Other unit types could have mini skill trees at elite tier

### Equipment Slots
- Units could equip weapon + armor + accessory
- Equipment found as rewards or crafted
- Risk of visual complexity on the board

### Unit Evolution
- Units could evolve into different branches
- Example: Scout → either "Pathfinder" (bonus movement) or "Tracker" (bonus vs isolated enemies)
- Adds replayability but requires balancing

## Narrative & Presentation

### Story Events
- Between-battle choices with narrative consequences
- Could grant temporary buffs, recruit unique units, or trigger special battles
- Example: "A local farmer offers to guide you through the pass. [Accept: safe passage, but no reward from next battle] [Decline: standard battle]"

### Cutscenes
- Minimal — maybe simple illustrated slides with text
- Voice acting would be expensive — text-only is fine
- Not needed for MVP

### Music & Sound
- Historical-inspired soundtrack (strings, percussion, horns)
- Battle sounds — clashing, shouting, war cries
- Ambient — wind, desert, caves

## Technical Ideas

### Replay System
- Record battles for review
- Could be shared as shareable replays
- Low priority

### Mod Support
- Community-made units, scenarios, campaigns
- Very far future

### Online Leaderboards
- Fastest run, least casualties, etc.
- Could be tied to daily challenges

## Scrapped / Rejected Ideas

- **Kingdom management** — too complex, distracts from tactical core
- **Base building** — doesn't fit David's fugitive narrative
- **Multiplayer** — doubles dev time, not worth it for solo dev
- **Open world** — scope creep, would take years
- **RTS elements** — real-time doesn't fit the tactical board game feel
- **Magic / fantasy** — violates historical grounding pillar

---

> *Remember: Just because it's in this file doesn't mean it should be built. Every feature must earn its place by making the tactical decisions more interesting.*
